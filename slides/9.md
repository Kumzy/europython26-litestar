# FastAPI / Django - opinionated

::left::

<div class="mt-4">
  <p class="font-bold text-lg mb-1">FastAPI</p>
  <ul class="stars muted mb-4">
    <li>Decorator-driven, works out of the box</li>
    <li>Config &amp; structure choices are limited; DI is global-ish</li>
  </ul>
  <p class="font-bold text-lg mb-1">Django</p>
  <ul class="stars muted">
    <li>Batteries included, conventions everywhere</li>
    <li>Monolithic, you adopt the whole worldview</li>
  </ul>
</div>

::right::

<div v-click>
<ul class="stars text-lg">
<li><strong>Fast to start, framework-shaped forever</strong></li>
<li class="muted">The defaults decide for you. It's great, until they don't fit</li>
<li class="muted">Then you fight the framework instead of composing around it</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="mt-5" v-click>
<p class="mb-1 font-bold gold">The catch:</p>
<p class="muted">Convenience is borrowed against future flexibility.</p>
</div>
